Free and Low-Cost Attractions

Milan, Italy is filled with free and low-cost attractions that won't break the bank. From the iconic Duomo di Milano to the Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II, there are plenty of sights to see without spending a dime. Here are some of the top locations:

  • Duomo di Milano - is a stunning cathedral and the most iconic landmark of Milan. With its Gothic architecture and impressive size, it is a must-visit attraction for anyone traveling to Milan. Located at Piazza del Duomo, 20122 Milano MI, Italy.
  • Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II - is a stunning shopping arcade in the heart of Milan. It is one of the oldest shopping malls in the world, and it is filled with luxurious boutiques and cafes. Located at Piazza del Duomo, 20123 Milano MI, Italy.
  • Sforza Castle - is a magnificent fortress in Milan that now houses several museums and art collections. It is a great place to explore Milan's history and culture. Located at Piazza Castello, 20121 Milano MI, Italy.
  • Brera Art Gallery - is a prestigious art museum in Milan that is home to an impressive collection of Italian Renaissance art. Located at Via Brera, 28, 20121 Milano MI, Italy.
  • Navigli Canal District - is a charming neighborhood in Milan known for its picturesque canals, historic buildings, and vibrant nightlife. Located at Navigli, 20144 Milano MI, Italy.
  • Palazzo Litta Cultural Hub - is a historic building in Milan that houses a contemporary art and design exhibition space, as well as a variety of events and workshops. Located at Corso Magenta, 24, 20123 Milano MI, Italy.
  • Milan Cathedral - is a magnificent Gothic cathedral in the heart of Milan. Visitors can explore the cathedral's impressive interior, including its many ornate sculptures and stained glass windows. Located at Piazza del Duomo, 20122 Milano MI, Italy.
  • San Maurizio al Monastero Maggiore - is a church and former monastery known for its beautiful frescoes and artwork. Located at Corso Magenta, 15, 20123 Milano MI, Italy.
  • Colonne di San Lorenzo - is a square and outdoor meeting place located near the Basilica di San Lorenzo, featuring ancient Roman columns and a lively atmosphere. Located at Piazza di San Lorenzo, 20121 Milano MI, Italy.
All of these attractions offer low-cost options for visitors. Some may have entry fees for certain exhibitions or guided tours, but entry to the main attraction is typically free or low-cost.
